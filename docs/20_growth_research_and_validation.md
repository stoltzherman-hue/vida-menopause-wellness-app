# Growth, Research, and Validation

## Market note

Founder-provided market numbers should be treated as assumptions until verified with primary or reputable market sources. The product should be built with a research backlog and validation plan rather than hard-coded claims.

## Validation questions

1. Do users want community, AI companion, or tracking first?
2. Which symptoms drive willingness to pay?
3. Does doctor-report export create premium conversion?
4. Does free community increase retention?
5. Do users trust AI for menopause support if safety boundaries are clear?
6. What language feels respectful rather than patronizing?

## Acquisition channels

- SEO content by symptom and stage.
- Community-led growth.
- Expert webinars.
- Partnerships with menopause coaches and clinicians.
- Employer menopause support programs later.
- Podcast collaborations.
- Social video education.
- Referral loops.

## Activation loops

- Onboarding -> personalized plan.
- First check-in -> first insight.
- First community post -> replies.
- First doctor report -> premium value.

## Referral loop

- Free community invite.
- Circle invite.
- Shareable educational resources.
- "Invite a friend who gets it" flow.

## Research backlog

- Interview 20 perimenopause users.
- Interview 10 post-menopause users.
- Interview 5 clinicians/menopause specialists.
- Test onboarding copy.
- Test premium willingness-to-pay.
- Test AI companion trust and tone.
- Test community anonymity preference.

## Risk: crowded femtech market

The claim that there is only one competitor should not be used externally. Competitors and adjacent players include telehealth menopause platforms, period apps adding menopause features, community forums, health trackers, and general AI companions.
